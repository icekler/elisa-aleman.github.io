Efforts during my time in school

I have belonged to the same laboratory since I was an undergraduate. In that laboratory, I participated in various projects and researches and became a team leader in some of them. All of them were difficult, but I got to finish them thanks to teamwork, pressure from deadlines, somehow managing to produce results each time. Thanks to that, I was able to make presentations at academic conferences and participate in competitions. However, I started my own private project when I was an undergraduate. Since I was an undergraduate, I have been thinking of publishing the research results and submitting them to international academic journals. Regrettably, it wasn't accepted in the journals I chose at first, so I continued to refine the same research during my master's course, continue to produce results, and challenge myself to publish. Unfortunately, I was rejected about eight times. Five years later, I challenged myself to try to get it published for the ninth time came. I thought I would give up several times, but I ended refining it towards its best version ever, and the results and methods convinced the reviewers. After overcoming the difficulties and staying up several nights and getting the reviewers to approve, I was told by the editors that it needed futher revision. I was alone. I am the eldest senior in the laboratory, and the juniors who worked with me previously on this project had already graduated. My academic advisor helped me, but it wasn't enough to help the feelings of helplessness. Looking back, I don't know what I would have done without my advisor's strength, but I was able to continue because of that strength.

Finally, five years after starting this research, I recieved the notice of acceptance. Looking back on myself, who had thought about giving up many times, now I think that it was right not to give up. I also learned how much I grew up over the five years by reading the previous versions of my paper. And I realized it was I who saw this to the end, and I was able to take pride in it. I would like to celebrate with the joy of being accepted, but I feel that there will be harder things that I should not give up even if I want to further in the future. I still have job hunting ahead of me, and I haven't finished my dissertation yet. From now on, I will have to overcome even bigger walls many times. But now, thinking about this moment, I decided to just keep in mind that I shouldn't give up. As a result, I have currently published two peer-reviewed international academic journals as the first author, one national academic journal, three international conference presentations, and co-authored in five international conferences.

My image of the future

I made an effort to find myself, so I dream of myself in the future when I find a place where I can work the way I am. I would like to work in a place where I can develop new technologies, discover new knowledge, and freely express ideas that arise from fresh perspectives and diversity. I think that a job that allows me to live anywhere by remote work, or a global workplace where I'd work in collaboration with people from various places, is also ideal for me. My philosophy is I don't live to work, but rather work to live. Therefore, rather than exhausting myself, I want to work for a company that I can go back home thinking I have gained something in my life after the day is over. And since I can speak various languages and have various skills not limited to IT, I would like to strive for a place where they are not wasted. Most of the things I'm studying in my doctoral course use natural language processing, but I'm also interested in various languages, so I think it's important that the languages I can speak are useful for my work. I would like to improve this world little by little by creating new AI infused with my own skills. I would like to develop something that benefits people. The reason I write this is that information engineering and AI, in particular, have recently been used in a way that makes people distrustful. But I believe that AI and information engineering have the power to improve the world. I want to be a version of myself who can bring that power to the world as much as possible.
